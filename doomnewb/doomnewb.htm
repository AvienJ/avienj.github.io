Doom is a video game series and media franchise created by John Carmack, John Romero, Adrian Carmack, Kevin Cloud, and Tom Hall. The series focuses on the exploits of an unnamed space marine operating under the auspices of the Union Aerospace Corporation, who fights hordes of demons and the undead.W


Ever since Doom's release in 1993, Doom's Community and methods of playing doom have changed drasticly. This guide should hopefully simplify the process of playing Doom along with it's sequal, Installing mods, and playing online.


Aquiring Doom

The easiest way to Aquire Doom right now is through video game distribution services such as Steam or GOG. Through Steam you can choose to play doom normally or through DOSBox. DOSBox lets you play Doom with a classic hardware and graphic emulator if you wanted to play in MS-DOS. This is the most straight forward way of playing Doom. But mods may not work unless you have source ports (discussed later).


General Knowledge

Doom has many of its own original words. Here is a table of some common ones:


Word Definition
WAD An abbreviation of "Where's All the Data". It is a file that contains game content.
IWAD A WAD that can stand on it's own as it's own game.
PWAD A WAD that modifies a IWAD's content
.deh deh is short for DeHackEd, .deh files were a very earily of modifying Doom and is still used to this day.
Megawad A PWAD that replaces all or most of Doom's levels with custom levels.
Total conversion A total conversion is a Doom mod that modifies Doom to be unrecognisable to the original Doom. With it's own levels, graphics, and more.

Source Ports

Source ports are conversions of the original source code to add in extra features like: Higher resolutions, In-game customizable controls, and Mods. Source ports require a bit of setup though, First you will want to select your source port. There a variety of soure ports that each have their different traits and features. Here is a list of of the most common ones:


GZDoom

GZDoom(or ZDoom) is the most latest and active source port out there. It almost supports all mods and all IWAD's, This will most likely be your main source port for singleplayer.

To learn more and download, goto zdoom.org

Zandronum

Zandronum is a multiplayer version of GZDoom. It still includes features like high resolutions, But also included features like: Servers, Medals, Custom content, and more. This will most likely be your main port for multiplayer.

To learn more and download, goto zandronum.com

Chocolate Doom

Chocolate doom is a source port that keeps as close to the original gameplay as possible, while fixing bugs and running on modern computers. Most newer mods most likely won't work, but much older level pack most often work.

To learn more and download, goto chocolate-doom.org

Launching Doom/Mods


Using GZDoom

First, download GZDoom from zdoom.com. Now, use an extraction software such as WinRAR or 7-Zip to extract the content into a empty folder.

Now, open your File Explorer and goto "C:\Program Files (x86)\Steam\steamapps\common\Ultimate Doom\base" if you bought the Steam version or goto "C:\GOG Games\DOOM" if you bought the GOG version and copy DOOM.wad to your GZDoom folder. You can also use Freedoom, a free version of Doom and Doom 2.

You can run gzdoom.exe if you want to play without mods and it should automatically load up or give a selection of IWAD's to choose from.

Mods or modifications are PWAD's created by the community or a third-party. Ever since Doom released, the modding community grew and the tools used to maker Doom mods became even easier. That's why there are over 100,000 mods to play.

if you want to play with mods then, find your mod of chose. You can find mods at: ModDB or you could find mods through the forums of Doomworld. Once you have downloaded your mod, extract the contents of the mod into your GZDoom folder, I will be using Brutal Doom. Doom mods will usually be a .wad or .pk3 file.

Now you can drag and drop the mod file onto gzdoom.exe and it should load the mod

And wallah, GZDoom should launch with your mod loaded, now you can goto options and configure your resolution, controls, and more!

Using Zandronum

First, download Zandronum from zandronum.com and run installer. It should automatically install for you and create shortcuts on your desktop and in your start menu. It is recommended that you copy your Doom wad to C:\Program Files (x86)\Zandronum\ so that it will automatically load Doom as your IWAD. There should be 2 shortcuts on your desktop/start menu, one lets you player Zandronum in singleplayer, the other one opens a server browser that lets you play online! Zandronum's settings are very similar of to GZDoom's settings and most mods are compatible.

Using Chocolate Doom

First, download Chocolate Doom from chocolate-doom.org and extract it just like gzdoom and also extract your wads to your chocolate doom folder. I would recommend only having one wad in the chocolate doom folder at once, since there is no wad selection menu. To load mods with chocolate doom you will have to use the cmd, to open the cmd in your current folder simply type cmd in the address bar. Then type "chocolate-doom.exe -file [Your PWAD filename]" with no brackets. To configure settings, run chocolate-doom-setup.exe

Doom 2

Doom 2 is a heavily modified version of Doom, with new levels, monsters, weapons, and more. Doom 2 can be played in the exact Doom can be played. Just copy the Doom 2 wad to the same places you copied the Doom wad. You will most likely need Doom 2 for most mods and almost all multiplayer servers. You can also get Doom 2 on Steam and GOG.
